## Framework: The Messaging Matrix

This playbook organizes approaches across two axes:

| | **Low Commitment** | **High Commitment** |
|---|---|---|
| **Warm / Personal** | A1–A5 | B1–B5 |
| **Professional / Structured** | C1–C5 | D1–D5 |

Each cell contains a distinct angle, tone, and ask. Mix and match based on:
- How well you know her
- Her risk tolerance
- Her current pain points
- The channel (text, call, coffee, email)

## WARM / PERSONAL — LOW COMMITMENT (A1–A5)

These feel like a friend sharing something useful. No pitch. No ask. Just signal.

### A1: The "I Made You Something" Text

**Channel:** iMessage / WhatsApp / Signal  
**Tone:** Casual, helpful, no strings attached  
**Goal:** Get her to read the HB 1292 checklist and think "this is useful"

```
Hey [name] — I've been studying Georgia's new notary law (HB 1292, the 
journal/training/ID thing that went live in January). I noticed there's 
no good reference guide out there, so I had an AI compile a full 
compliance checklist with examples, self-filer rules, and a printable 
quick-reference card. 

Want me to send it over? No charge, no pitch — just thought you'd 
find it useful since you're in the trenches with this stuff.
```

**Follow-up (if she says yes):**
```
Attached. Also — how's the journal thing working for you in practice? 
Are you handwriting everything or did you find an electronic option 
you like?
```

**Why it works:** You're the friend who noticed a problem and solved it. The follow-up question is the real opening — it gets her talking about her workflow pain.

---

### A2: The "I Noticed Something Weird" Text

**Channel:** iMessage / casual text  
**Tone:** Curious, slightly conspiratorial  
**Goal:** Spark a conversation about Georgia's notary fee structure

```
Did you know Georgia notaries have the lowest fees in the country? 
$2 per signature. Tied with New York. Meanwhile Florida is $10, 
California is $15, Maine is $25. 

I was looking at this for a project and couldn't believe it. How 
do you even make money at $2? Is it all travel fees and loan 
signings?
```

**Why it works:** Everyone loves being the expert on their own industry. You're asking her to explain her world to you. She'll correct you, educate you, and in the process reveal her business model.

### A3: The "Saw This and Thought of You" Link Share

**Channel:** Text, social DM, or email  
**Tone:** Observational, light  
**Goal:** Position you as someone who pays attention to her industry

```
Saw this article about Texas making notary misconduct a criminal 
offense (up to a year in jail, felony for real estate fraud). 
https://notaryhub.com/articles/tx-ron-law-changes-2025

Made me think of you because Georgia hasn't gone that far yet, 
but HB 1292 already tightened up journal requirements and ID 
rules. Are you feeling the squeeze from the new law, or is it 
just business as usual?
```

**Why it works:** You're not selling anything. You're sharing intel. The question at the end is the hook — it invites her to vent or explain, both of which are valuable intel.

### A4: The "Coffee Shop Conversation Starter"

**Channel:** In person, casual setting  
**Tone:** Genuinely curious, not performative  
**Goal:** Understand her daily friction

```
"So I know you're a notary, but I actually have no idea what 
that means day-to-day. Like, walk me through your last signing. 
Where were you? What was the document? What took the most time?"
```

**Listen for:**
- Driving all over Atlanta
- 100-page loan packages with missing signatures
- Handwriting journal entries
- Clients showing up with expired IDs
- The pressure to not mess up a closing

**Your response (after she answers):**
```
"That sounds like a lot of administrative work for $2 a signature. 
What if AI could do the document pre-check, auto-fill the journal, 
and flag the blank fields before you even leave the house? Would 
that be useful, or does that sound like overkill?"
```

**Why it works:** She talks, you listen. The idea is seeded as a hypothetical, not a proposal.

### A5: The "Hypothetical Brainstorm" Text

**Channel:** Text or voice note  
**Tone:** Playful, speculative  
**Goal:** Test appetite for a partnership without naming it

```
Random thought: you know how notaries make basically nothing on 
the actual notarial act but decent money on loan signings and 
travel? What if there was a tool that made the loan signing part 
faster — like auto-scanning the package for blank notary blocks 
and pre-filling the journal — so you could do 6 signings a day 
instead of 3?

Not asking you to build anything. Just wondering if that's even 
a problem worth solving.
```

**Why it works:** You're floating an idea, not making an ask. If she says "yes, that's exactly my problem," you have permission to go deeper. If she says "nah, I don't do enough volume," you learned something.

## WARM / PERSONAL — HIGH COMMITMENT (B1–B5)

These involve a direct pitch of the joint venture idea, framed as a partnership between friends.

### B1: The "I Have an Idea for Us" Voice Note

**Channel:** Voice note (2–3 minutes)  
**Tone:** Energetic, personal, slightly vulnerable  
**Goal:** Make the pitch feel like a shared adventure, not a transaction

```
[Voice note transcript]

"Hey [name]. So I've been sitting on something and I want to run 
it by you because you're the only person I know who actually 
understands this space. 

I've spent the last year building AI governance infrastructure — 
like tamper-evident audit trails, compliance receipts, stuff 
that keeps lawyers and accountants out of trouble. And the whole 
time I've been thinking... this is exactly what notaries need. 

Texas just made notary misconduct a crime. Ohio added tech fees 
to RON. Georgia passed HB 1292 with journal requirements. The 
regulation is getting heavier, but notaries are still working 
with paper notebooks and Excel. 

Here's my thought: you bring the notary expertise, the commission, 
the industry credibility, the network of other notaries. I bring 
the AI infrastructure and the technical execution. We build a 
compliance copilot for Georgia notaries together. 

Not asking for money. Not asking for a ton of time. Just asking 
if you'd be open to a 30-minute call to see if this is even 
interesting. No pressure if it's not."
```

**Why it works:** Voice notes feel intimate and unscripted. The "you're the only person I know" frames her as uniquely qualified, not just convenient.

**Why it works:** She touches something physical (the checklist) before hearing the abstract idea. The distinction between "for you" and "with you" is critical.

**Channel:** Coffee or phone call  
**Tone:** Provocative, slightly challenging

**If she scores low (which she likely will):**

```
$2-per-signature trap are the ones who build systems. 

I want to help you build the system. Not just for you — for 
every Georgia notary. That's a business, not a side hustle."
```

**Why it works:** The founder check is diagnostic, not insulting. It creates cognitive dissonance between where she is and where she could be.

### B5: The "What If We Don't?" Fear-Based Pitch

**Channel:** In person or phone  
**Tone:** Serious, not alarmist  
**Goal:** Make inaction feel riskier than action

```
"I want to talk about something that could be uncomfortable, 
but I think it's important. 

Texas just made notary misconduct a Class A misdemeanor — up 
to a year in jail. Georgia hasn't gone that far, but HB 1292 
already expanded GSCCCA's oversight authority. 

You're now legally required to maintain journals with 6 specific 
fields for self-filers. If you miss one, and that deed gets 
challenged in court, your journal is subpoenaed. If it's 
incomplete, you're the weak link.

Every notary in Georgia is one bad journal entry away from a 
compliance nightmare. And right now, there's no tool built 
for Georgia's specific requirements.

I can build that tool. I have the infrastructure. What I need 
is someone who understands the stakes from the inside.

I'm asking you to be that person. Not because I need a notary 
— because I need someone who won't let me build something 
stupid or impractical."
```

**Why it works:** Fear sells when it's specific and actionable, not vague. The closing reframes her role as "protector of quality," not just "domain expert."

## CHANNEL-SPECIFIC TACTICS

### Text / iMessage
- **Best for:** A1–A5 (warm, low-commitment)
- **Length:** 2–4 short paragraphs max
- **Timing:** Weekday evening or weekend afternoon (not business hours)
- **Follow-up rule:** If no response in 48 hours, send one nudge max. Then wait.

### Voice Note
- **Best for:** B1, B3, B5
- **Length:** 90 seconds–3 minutes
- **Tone:** Conversational, slightly unpolished (perfection feels rehearsed)
- **Script tip:** Write a transcript, practice once, then record. Don't read verbatim.

### Email
- **Best for:** C2, B4, D2, D4
- **Subject lines:**
  - "Georgia notaries just got regulated harder — and that's good news"
  - "Free HB 1292 compliance tool — want to beta test?"
  - "30-day experiment: Georgia notary AI tool"
  - "Numbers on a notary AI business — worth a look?"
- **Length:** 150–250 words for warm-up; 400–600 for full pitch
- **CTA:** One clear ask. Never two.

### In Person / Coffee
- **Best for:** A4, B2, B3, B5, D1, D3, D5
- **Prep:** Bring one physical artifact (laminated checklist, printed one-pager, competitor screenshot)
- **Flow:** 70% her talking, 30% you talking
- **Closing:** "No need to decide now. Sleep on it. I'll follow up Thursday."

### LinkedIn
- **Best for:** C1
- **Cadence:** 2–3 weeks of engagement before DM
- **Content:** Share HB 1292 insights, tag Georgia notaries, comment on industry posts

## OBJECTION HANDLING

### "I'm not technical. I can't build software."

**Response:** "That's exactly why this works. You don't build anything. You tell me what notaries actually need, test what I build, and introduce me to people who would use it. I handle all the tech. You're the domain expert, not the engineer."
